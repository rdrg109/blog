1 Introduction

This blog post presents two Youtube playlists which I find valuable for someone whose major is Computer Science.

2 Youtube playlists

The Youtube playlists, which I'd like to share, are published by the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) and they are presented below

  • ACM A.M. Turing Award Laureate Interviews
  • ACM A.M. Turing Award Lectures

I've noticed that there are some videos which contain very few visits. Because of this, the next section presents a table which shows the number of views, number of likes, number of dislikes for each video in those playlists. The Usefulness of the videos section under the Personal thoughts section presents my thoughts on why I think these playlists are valuable.

4 Personal thoughts

4.1 Movitation for creating this blog post

I've mainly created this blog post to share this Youtube playlist with my colleagues (undergraduate computer science students) and because I felt that some of those videos need to have more attention, since some of them have less than 100 visits.

I noticed that some of the videos had very few visits when I recalled that I had watched an interview to D. Knuth. I started searching that video, found it on Youtube, and then Youtube recommended this video to Barbara Liskov which only has 64 views, so I asked myself: How is it come that a video of an interview to someone which have made a great contribution to the Computer Science community has very few views?

4.2 Usefulness of the videos

The videos at the "ACM A.M. Turing Award Lectures" playlist are useful because the lecture is given by someone which has won a Turing Award, so their explanation might provide useful information not only on the topic but in the Computer Science area

The videos at the "ACM A.M. Turing Award Laureate Interviews" playlist are useful because you get to know a winner of a Turing Award to a deeper extent which might be of help when taking decisions in your Computer Science career. I mention this because in the interviews I've watched, Donald Knuth and Barbara Liskov, they mention some of the personal decisions they made in their lives.
